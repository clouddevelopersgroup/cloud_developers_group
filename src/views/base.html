January Meeting Topic: "Hello, World." on Google App Engine with Python

First in a series of "Hello, World." presentations.

We'll cover the basics to get an actual site up and running on Google App Engine with a little bit of Python for good measure.

  • Setup environment for development on the App Engine.
  • Register for App Engine.
  • Create project on the site.
  • Install Python.
  • Get the App Engine SDK.
  • Install NetBeans.
  • Setup the project on your development machine.
  • Develop a basic To-Do site.
  • Optionally, deploy the site to App Engine.

Presenter: Michael D. Hall

Mike has been a professional software developer for over a decade now. Primarily he's focused on Microsoft technologies but has been passionately learning about Google App Engine.

Our January meeting is planned to be held at Crystal Lake Public Library.

Review: Inaugural Meeting

Well, we're finally underway! And a good start it is. We started the meeting off with a short introduction to the group and some ideas on what direction we could take the group. Afterward we discussed some of the topics we'd like to see at the meetings and even got volunteers for the next three meetings!

First things first! All people are welcome. This is a no-pressure group. You do not have to feel like you have to present or even talk at the meetings. Come, hang out and soak up the info!

Audience: Software developers and anyone interested in learning more about the various cloud platforms and related technologies.

Group Goal: Share information on the various cloud platforms and related technologies. Collaborate on group projects that provide opportunities to develop concrete experience with cloud technologies. Treat members with respect and an open-minded attitude to sharing what we learn.

Meeting Schedule: Second Monday of the month.

  • January 11th, 2010
    • Introduction to the Google App Engine Platform.
    • Michael D. Hall, just3ws
  • February 8th, 2010
    • Introduction to the Microsoft Azure Platform.
    • TBD, Microsoft
  • March 8th, 2010
    • Introduction to the Amazon Cloud Platform.
    • Ryan Gerry, Google
  • April 12th, 2010
    • TENTATIVE: Group Project Codename: "Nerdtersect"
    • Michael D. Hall, just3ws

Meeting Format: Typically meetings will follow a traditional Intro-Presentation-Discussion format. But we'll also have workshop meetings where everyone will be encouraged to bring their laptop and collaborate on some code.

Topics: We are open to any topic as long as it relates to supporting development against a cloud platform. We could have a meeting on anything from learning Django and Python for AppEngine to Parsing the Platfom Pricing Structures. All topics will be considered!

Online Presence: We have accounts on Google Groups, GitHub, Eventbrite and Twitter. We even have our own domain and website: http://www.clouddevelopersgroup.com so we shouldn't be hard to find.

Organizers: Mike Hall is the group organizer and the point of contact for all things group related. If you'd like to propose a topic, volunteer to help out with the meetings or have any questions about the group you can contact him directly at mhall@clouddevelopersgroup.com.

Get Involved!: Volunteer, Sponsor, Speak, Ideas! We would like to find a permanent home for the group, ideally a location with a meeting room, wireless internet access and a projector. If you would like to donate food, drinks or equipment to the group please contact Mike Hall at mhall@clouddevelopersgroup.com.

I'm very excited about the future of this group! 2010 is going to be a fun year!
